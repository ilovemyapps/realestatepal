### **Tenant Protection Resources**
Since most San Mateo County cities do not have independent Rent Boards, they rely on state law AB 1482 for tenant protections:

**State-Level Tenant Protections (applies to most cities)**:
- **AB 1482 Tenant Protection Act**: Limits annual rent increases to 5%+CPI or 10% (whichever is lower)
- **County Tenant Rights Information**: https://www.smcgov.org/housing/tenants-protections-and-rights

## Important Notes

1. **All links verified** as current official government websites as of August 2025
2. **ADU Approval Process**: Most cities have adopted ministerial approval; conforming ADUs only require building permits
3. **Fee Waivers**: ADUs under 750 square feet typically exempt from impact fees
4. **Pre-approved Designs**: Multiple cities offer pre-approved ADU design plans to expedite approval
5. **Minimum Rental Terms**: All ADUs and JADUs require minimum 30-day rental periods
6. **Owner Occupancy Requirements**: Per state law, no owner occupancy requirements until January 1, 2025
7. **GIS/Property Maps**: Most cities use the county's unified system; some larger cities have their own GIS systems
8. **Tenant Protections**: San Mateo County relies on state law AB 1482 rather than city-level Rent Boards (except Half Moon Bay)

Contact individual city planning departments for current fee schedules and specific requirements. The San Mateo County ADU Resource Center (https://smcadu.org/) provides comprehensive support services for the entire county.
